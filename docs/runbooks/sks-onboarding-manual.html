For everyone

Your first 2 minutes

This is the whole thing. No accounts to create, no passwords to invent.

1

A message arrives from SKS

A text or email with one link: “Set up your login — takes 2 minutes.”

2

Tap the link

It opens your EQ login. No app to download — it works in your phone’s browser.

3

Pick a PIN

Something you’ll remember — 4 to 12 characters, a number or a short word, your choice.

4

You’re in

Straight to your SKS apps — your details, your licences, your jobs.

5

Add a second step (managers & supervisors first)

Soon after, we’ll ask you to add a second sign-in step — the same idea as your banking app. 30 seconds, then it’s automatic.

Forgot your PIN later?

Tap “Forgot PIN” on the sign-in screen, or ask your manager for a reset link. Set a new PIN — your old one stops working straight away.

For the office

Setting up your team

End to end, a morning’s work plus a short pilot. You never see or handle anyone’s PIN.

SKS title / bandRoleWhat it grants
Directors, GM, Operations & Project / Contracts Managers, office & contracts leadsmanagerEverything, plus inviting and managing people
Site Supervisors, Foremen, Leading HandssupervisorTeam oversight; can’t manage logins
Electricians, Technicians, Installers, Estimators, general Office / AdminemployeeStandard day-to-day access
Apprentices, TraineesapprenticeStandard access, flagged as developing
Labour hire, subcontractors, casualslabour_hireLowest access, meant to be time-bound

Rule of thumb: role follows job title. The only judgement call is “is this person a lead?” — if yes, manager; if no, employee.

  1. Build the staff list. One row per person: email, first name, role, which apps.Most field staff just need Field + Cards.
  2. Send invites in bulk. Paste the list into the bulk-invite screen. Each person gets a one-time, 7-day link.
  3. Pilot with ~5 people first. A manager, a supervisor, a couple of field staff. Confirm they get in cleanly.
  4. Switch on automatic emails. After the pilot, invites and reset links send themselves.
  5. Roll out to everyone. Run the full list; re-send a reminder to anyone who hasn’t set up.
  6. When someone leaves: deactivate. Access stops on their next click, company-wide. Their record stays for history.
For decision-makers

Why this is safe

The same simplicity that helps a tradie is what makes it defensible.

No emailed passwords, ever

Everyone sets their own PIN via a one-time, time-limited link. Nothing secret to intercept.

A second lock where it counts

Managers and supervisors use a second sign-in step — on by policy, with a humane runway.

Instant offboarding

A departing staff member loses access on their next click — not at the end of the day.

Everything is recorded

Sign-ins, invites, resets and role changes are written to an audit trail.

Locked to SKS

People only ever see SKS information — no chance of straying into another company’s data.

Brute-force resistant

Repeated bad sign-in attempts are automatically slowed and blocked.
